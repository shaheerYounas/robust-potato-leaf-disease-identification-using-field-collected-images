========================================================
  EDA GRAPHS -- SIMPLE EXPLANATIONS
  Potato Leaf Disease Dataset (Uncontrolled Environment)
========================================================

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 1 - 01_class_distribution.png
         "How many images are in each disease category?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A bar chart. Each bar is one disease type (Fungi, Pest,
  Bacteria, etc.). The height of the bar tells you how
  many photos exist for that disease.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  The bars are very unequal. Fungi has 748 photos but
  Nematode has only 68. This huge gap is a problem -- if
  you train an AI on this data, it will barely learn what
  Nematode looks like because it has seen so few examples.


  "Some diseases are very well represented in the dataset,
   others are barely there, the data is imbalanced."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 2 -- 02_image_size_distribution.png
         "Are all photos the same size?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A histogram (bar chart of counts) showing the width and
  height of every image in the dataset.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  All 3,076 images are exactly 1500 x 1500 pixels. There
  is only one bar and it covers all images -- no variation.


  "Every single photo is the same size, so we only need
   one simple resize step before training."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 3 -- 03_pixel_intensity_histogram.png
         "How bright or dark are the photos overall?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A histogram of pixel brightness values (0 = black,
  255 = white). It shows how many pixels fall into each
  brightness level across ALL images.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  The curve sits in the middle range (not too dark, not
  too bright). There are no peaks near 0 (pitch black)
  or 255 (completely washed out). This is good -- it
  means lighting conditions are reasonably consistent.

  "The photos are neither too dark nor too bright --
   the lighting is fairly consistent across the dataset."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 4 -- 04_sample_images.png
         "What do the actual leaf photos look like?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A grid of real photos -- 5 random images from each of
  the 7 disease classes, so 35 photos total.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  You can see with your own eyes what each disease looks
  like. Some photos are clear and sharp; others look
  blurry or have messy backgrounds with soil and stems.
  This is exactly what "uncontrolled environment" means --
  the photos were taken in real fields, not a lab.


  "Real field photos -- some are clear, some are blurry,
   taken in natural messy conditions."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 5 -- 05_blur_distribution.png
         "How blurry are the photos?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A histogram showing a "blur score" (called Laplacian
  variance) for every image. A LOW score means very blurry;
  a HIGH score means sharp and clear. The dashed red line
  marks the threshold we set (score = 100) -- anything
  below that line is considered blurry.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  A large chunk of images piles up on the left (low scores),
  meaning 41.7% of all photos are blurry. This is a serious
  problem because a blurry photo does not show the disease
  features clearly -- the AI cannot learn properly from it.


  "Nearly half the dataset is blurry, which will hurt
   model accuracy if not fixed before training."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 6 -- 06_class_imbalance.png
         "How unequal are the class sizes? (percentage view)"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A bar chart showing each class as a percentage of the
  total dataset, making it easy to compare their shares.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  Fungi takes up 24% of the data; Nematode takes up just
  2.2%. The imbalance ratio is 11x -- meaning the biggest
  class has 11 times more photos than the smallest.
  Any AI trained on this will be biased towards predicting
  the common diseases and will miss the rare ones.


  "The biggest class is 11 times larger than the smallest --
   the dataset is severely imbalanced."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 7 -- 08_background_complexity.png
         "How messy or busy are the photo backgrounds?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  Two bar charts side by side.
  Left: Background variance -- how much visual "noise"
        (soil, stems, other plants) is in the background.
  Right: Edge density -- how many sharp edges/lines exist
         in the image (leaves with complex textures score
         high here).

WHAT IT MEANS:
  Bacteria photos have the most cluttered backgrounds
  (variance = 3,712) -- lots of distracting background
  detail. Healthy and Phytopthora leaves have the most
  edges, meaning their texture is complex and detailed.
  Complex backgrounds make it harder for AI to focus on
  the leaf itself.


  "Some disease classes have very messy backgrounds which
   can confuse the model during training."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 8 -- 09_hsv_brightness_analysis.png
         "Is brightness consistent across all disease types?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A box plot. Each box represents one disease class. The
  box shows where most brightness values sit; the lines
  (whiskers) show the full range; dots are outliers.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  All boxes are at roughly the same height and similar
  size. This means brightness does not vary much between
  classes -- the photos were all taken under similar
  lighting. The standard deviation is only 15.27
  (on a 0-255 scale), which is very low.


  "Lighting is almost the same across all disease types --
   brightness is not a problem in this dataset."

--------------------------------------------------------
GRAPH 9 -- 09b_saturation_vs_brightness.png
         "Are vivid green photos brighter than dull ones?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A scatter plot. Each dot is one image. The X-axis is
  Saturation (how vivid/colourful the leaf looks -- deep
  green vs pale or yellowed). The Y-axis is Brightness
  (how light or dark the photo is). Dots are colour-coded
  by disease class.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  The dots form a loose cloud with no strong pattern.
  High saturation does not automatically mean high
  brightness -- they move somewhat independently.
  This tells us that colour vividness and photo brightness
  are two different signals the AI can use separately.


  "Colourful leaves are not necessarily brighter ones --
   colour and brightness carry independent information."

--------------------------------------------------------
INLINE CHART A -- RGB Channel Bar Chart (Section 2)
         "Which colour channel is strongest in the photos?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  Three bars: Blue, Green, Red -- showing the average
  pixel value for each colour channel across all images.
  The error bars show how much variation there is.

WHAT IT MEANS:
  Green is clearly the tallest bar (mean = 139) because
  leaves are green. Blue is the lowest (101) because the
  sky and shadows add blue but leaves themselves don't.
  All three error bars are small -- the colour balance
  is consistent across photos.


  "Green dominates as expected for leaf images -- colour
   balance is stable across the whole dataset."

--------------------------------------------------------
INLINE CHART B -- Blurry % per Class Bar Chart (Section 3)
         "Which disease type has the most blurry photos?"
--------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT SHOWS:
  A horizontal bar chart. Each bar is a disease class.
  Bar length = percentage of photos in that class that
  are blurry. Red bars = above 40% blurry (problem zone).

WHAT IT MEANS:
  Nematode is the worst: 70.6% of its photos are blurry.
  Fungi (43.5%) and Bacteria (47.3%) are also in the
  red zone. These classes need the most cleanup --
  blurry images should be removed or filtered before
  training the AI.


  "Nematode, Fungi, and Bacteria have the most blurry
   photos -- these classes need the most cleaning."

========================================================
  SUMMARY -- KEY PROBLEMS TO DISCUSS
========================================================

  1. IMBALANCE (most urgent)
     Nematode has only 68 photos vs Fungi's 748.
     The AI will strongly favour common diseases.
     Fix: use weighted loss, oversample rare classes.

  2. BLURRY IMAGES (very important)
     41.7% of photos are too blurry to learn from.
     Fix: remove the worst images before training.

  3. COMPLEX BACKGROUNDS (moderate)
     Bacteria and Fungi photos have cluttered backgrounds.
     Fix: use background augmentation techniques.

  4. GOOD NEWS
     - All images are the same size (1500x1500) -- easy.
     - Lighting is consistent -- no exposure correction.
     - Green channel signal is strong and clean.
